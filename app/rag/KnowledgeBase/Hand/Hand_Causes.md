## Causes of Hand Fractures

### Common Causes

- Falls (most common)
- Punching hard objects (boxer’s fracture)
- Direct trauma or blows
- Crush injuries
- Motor vehicle accidents
- Sports injuries

---

### Special Case: Boxer’s Fracture

- Occurs when punching with a closed fist
- Affects the fifth metacarpal (little finger side)

---

### Risk Factors

- Contact sports (boxing, football)
- Osteoporosis (weak bones)
- High-impact activities
- Young adults (especially males in trauma cases)

---

### Key Insight

Hand fractures often result from sudden impact rather than gradual stress.
