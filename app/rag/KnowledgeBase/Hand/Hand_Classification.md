## Classification of Hand Fractures

Hand fractures are classified based on bone type and fracture pattern.

---

### 1. Based on Bone Involved

#### Metacarpal Fractures
- Occur in palm bones
- Most common (~40%)
- Includes boxer’s fracture (5th metacarpal)

#### Phalangeal Fractures
- Occur in finger bones
- Can involve:
  - Proximal
  - Middle
  - Distal phalanx

---

### 2. Based on Fracture Type

- Open vs Closed
- Displaced vs Non-displaced
- Simple vs Comminuted (multiple fragments)
- Intra-articular vs Extra-articular

---

### 3. Special Fractures

#### Boxer’s Fracture
- 5th metacarpal neck fracture

#### Bennett’s Fracture
- Base of thumb fracture with dislocation

#### Rolando Fracture
- Comminuted thumb fracture

---

### Key Insight

Rotational deformity is more important than angulation in hand fractures.
