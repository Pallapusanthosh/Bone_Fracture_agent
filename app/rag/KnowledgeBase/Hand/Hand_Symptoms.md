## Hand Fracture Symptoms

Common symptoms of a hand fracture include:

- Severe pain (worsens with movement or gripping)
- Swelling
- Bruising or discoloration
- Tenderness
- Deformity (crooked finger or abnormal shape)
- Stiffness or inability to move fingers
- Reduced grip strength
- Shortened or rotated finger (overlapping fingers)
- Numbness (possible nerve damage)

---

### Specific Signs

- Finger crossing over another when making a fist (rotational deformity)
- Sunken knuckle (common in boxer’s fracture)
- Difficulty using hand for daily tasks

---

### Emergency Signs

- Bone visible through skin (open fracture)
- Severe deformity
- Complete loss of movement
- Numbness or color change in fingers
