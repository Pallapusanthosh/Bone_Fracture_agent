## Treatment of Hand Fractures

Treatment depends on severity, alignment, and type.

---

### 1. Non-Surgical Treatment

Used for stable fractures:

- Splint or cast (3–6 weeks)
- Buddy strapping (finger support)
- Closed reduction (manual alignment)
- Ice and pain relief

---

### 2. Surgical Treatment

Required when:

- Fracture is displaced
- Rotational deformity present
- Open fracture
- Multiple fractures

#### Procedures:

- Internal fixation (pins, screws, plates)
- K-wire fixation
- External fixation (severe cases)

---

### 3. Special Case: Boxer’s Fracture

- Usually treated with splint/cast
- Surgery if severe displacement

---

### Key Insight

Early stabilization + proper alignment = better function recovery.
