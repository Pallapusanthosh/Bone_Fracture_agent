## Diagnosis of Hand Fractures

### Clinical Examination

Doctors check for:

- Swelling and bruising
- Deformity
- Finger alignment (rotation test)
- Ability to move fingers
- Tendon function
- Nerve damage (numbness)

---

### Functional Test (Important)

- Patient makes a fist
- Normal: fingers point toward scaphoid area
- Abnormal: overlapping or rotated fingers → fracture

---

### Imaging Techniques

#### X-ray (Primary)
- Confirms fracture
- Shows alignment and location

#### CT Scan
- Used for complex fractures
- Provides 3D detail

---

### Classification in Diagnosis

- Displaced → bones out of alignment
- Non-displaced → bones aligned
- Open → bone breaks skin
- Closed → no skin break

---

### Key Insight

Rotational deformity is critical → affects hand function significantly.
