## Shoulder Fracture Treatment

Treatment depends on severity, type, and displacement of the fracture.

### 1. Non-Surgical Treatment (Most common)
- Immobilization using sling or brace
- Ice packs to reduce swelling
- Pain medications
- Physical therapy after initial healing

Used for:
- Non-displaced fractures
- Mild injuries

### 2. Surgical Treatment

Required when:
- Bone fragments are significantly displaced
- Joint is involved
- Severe fracture patterns

#### Common procedures:
- ORIF (Open Reduction and Internal Fixation)
  - Metal plates, screws, or pins used to fix bones
- Shoulder replacement (Arthroplasty)
  - Used in severe joint damage cases

### 3. Recovery

- Healing time: Few weeks to several months
- Physical therapy is essential
- Possible long-term stiffness

### 4. Complications

- Stiffness
- Arthritis risk
- Malunion or nonunion
- Infection (especially in open fractures)
