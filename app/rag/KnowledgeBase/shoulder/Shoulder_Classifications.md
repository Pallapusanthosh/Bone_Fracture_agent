## Neer Classification of Proximal Humerus Fractures

The Neer classification is based on:
- Number of fracture parts
- Degree of displacement

### Key Thresholds:
- Displacement > 1 cm OR
- Angulation > 45°

If below these → considered non-displaced

---

### Fracture Parts:
The proximal humerus is divided into 4 parts:
1. Humeral head
2. Greater tubercle
3. Lesser tubercle
4. Humeral shaft

---

### Classification Types:

#### 1. One-Part Fracture
- No significant displacement (<1 cm and <45°)
- Most common (70–80%)
- Usually treated conservatively

#### 2. Two-Part Fracture
- One segment displaced (>1 cm or >45°)
- Common type (≈20%)

#### 3. Three-Part Fracture
- Two segments displaced
- Less common (≈5%)

#### 4. Four-Part Fracture
- All segments displaced
- Rare (<1%)
- High risk of complications (e.g., osteonecrosis)
- Usually requires surgery

---

### Key Insight:
Even if multiple fracture lines exist,
→ it is still classified as ONE-PART if no displacement.
