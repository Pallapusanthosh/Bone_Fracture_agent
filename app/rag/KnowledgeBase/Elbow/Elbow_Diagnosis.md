## Diagnosis of Elbow Fractures

### Clinical Examination

Doctors check:

- Swelling and tenderness
- Range of motion (flexion, extension)
- Deformity
- Neurovascular status (pulse, nerves)

---

### Important Functional Test

- Elbow extension test
- Inability to fully extend → possible fracture

---

### Neurovascular Check (Critical)

- Check pulse (brachial artery)
- Check nerves:
  - Median nerve
  - Radial nerve
  - Ulnar nerve

---

### Imaging

#### X-ray (Primary)

- Shows fracture location
- Detects displacement and angulation

---

#### Key Radiological Signs

- Posterior fat pad sign → hidden fracture
- Anterior humeral line disruption
- Radiocapitellar misalignment

---

#### Advanced Imaging

- CT scan → complex fractures
- MRI → soft tissue injuries

---

### Key Insight

A visible posterior fat pad on X-ray ALWAYS indicates pathology.
